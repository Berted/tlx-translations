Description

Jelix likes recently popular street food called "Es Kepal Moli" a lot. After receiving Jelix's Es Kepal Moli, one of his friends, Fingga got curious, about the method of making Es Kepal Moli. Surprisingly one of the ingredients used by Jelix is Moli branded milk.

Currently, there're $$$N$$$ Moli Milks in Jelix's fridge. Everyday Jelix will use $$$A$$$ Moli Milks to make Es Kepal Moli. To not run out of stock, Jelix buys $$$B$$$ Moli Milks every day and then store in the fridge. Surely, Jelix buys Moli Milk more than he uses each day. Fingga got curious after $$$K$$$ days, how many Moli Milks are in Jelix's fridge. Help Fingga to resolve it!

Input Format

Input starts with a single integer $$$T (1 \leq T \leq 100)$$$ the number of test cases. The next lines contain 4 integer $$$N$$$, $$$A$$$, $$$B$$$, and $$$K$$$ $$$(1 \leq A < B, N \leq 100; 1 \leq K \leq 100)$$$.

Output Format

For each test case, output "Kasus #$$$X$$$: $$$Y$$$", where $$$X$$$ is the case number (starting from 1), and $$$Y$$$ is the amount of milk in the fridge after $$$K$$$ days.

Sample Input

3
5 2 4 1
7 3 6 2
10 2 8 3

Sample Output

Kasus #1: 7
Kasus #2: 13
Kasus #3: 28
